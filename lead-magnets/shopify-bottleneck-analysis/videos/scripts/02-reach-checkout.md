## Purpose

Explain why shoppers who add to cart may not continue to checkout, and frame this as a confidence, cost, or friction problem.

## Core Message

"They're interested. Now the job is to remove doubt and get them cleanly into checkout."

## Selected Hook

"Most people treat cart abandonment like the customer simply changed their mind. But if shoppers add to cart and do not reach checkout, the report is pointing to a much smaller moment. They said 'maybe', then stopped before the next step. Why?"

## Diagnostic Arc

- Immediate reveal: these shoppers showed interest, so something interrupted momentum between cart and checkout.
- Diagnosis: the cart may be creating doubt, surprise cost, confusion, or friction.
- Cost of inaction: every cart addition that fails to reach checkout makes product-page wins and ad spend work harder than they should.
- Fix path: simplify the cart, make costs and next steps clear, and remove distractions blocking checkout.
- Paid next step: use the diagnostic to inspect the cart-to-checkout handoff properly.
- Close: "Book my $295 Shopify Fix-It Diagnostic."

## Likely Causes

- Shipping cost appears too late.
- Discount code friction.
- Cart drawer is unclear.
- Returns or delivery details are missing.
- Upsells or cross-sells distract from checkout.
- Checkout button is not obvious on mobile.

## First Checks

- Cart drawer and cart page on mobile.
- Shipping threshold messaging.
- Discount code behaviour.
- Checkout button visibility.
- Popups or upsells blocking progress.

## Next Action

Make the cart feel simple, clear, and safe.
