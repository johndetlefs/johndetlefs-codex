## Purpose

Explain why shoppers who add to cart but do not reach checkout are not simply "cart abandoners"; they have shown product desire, then started judging whether the vendor feels safe enough to buy from.

## Core Message

"They want the product. Now the job is risk reversal: remove the fear of hassle, hidden cost, disappointment, or regret, then make checkout obvious."

## Likely Causes

- Shipping cost or delivery detail appears too late.
- Delivery timing feels vague or unsupported.
- Returns or refunds are hard to find when the shopper wants reassurance.
- Total price clarity arrives too late.
- The cart drawer or cart page is unclear on mobile.
- Upsells, cross-sells, popups, bundles, or discount fields compete with the checkout action.
- The checkout button is not obvious, unambiguous, or technically reliable enough.

## First Checks

- Does the cart clearly reduce the buyer's fear of hassle, hidden cost, disappointment, or regret?
- Are delivery fees, delivery timing, returns, refunds, and total price clear before checkout?
- Does the cart make the vendor feel trustworthy enough to buy from?
- Is the checkout button clear, simple, obvious, and unambiguous?
- Are visibility, layout, or technical issues getting in the way of the customer who is ready to move?
- Do any secondary cart elements compete with the checkout action?

## Next Action

Reduce risk, make the next step obvious, and turn the cart back into a bridge the customer is willing to cross.
