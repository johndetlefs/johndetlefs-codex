## Reference Standard

Use `lead-magnets/shopify-bottleneck-analysis/videos/scripts/01-add-to-cart.md`, `lead-magnets/shopify-bottleneck-analysis/videos/scripts/02-reach-checkout.md`, and `lead-magnets/shopify-bottleneck-analysis/videos/scripts/03-checkout-completion.md` as the current quality bar for voice, stage psychology, pacing, Remotion notes, paid-step bridge, and close.

Reference lessons:

- Write for John's mouth: short spoken sentences, contractions, concrete claims, no strategy-document phrasing.
- Hooks need belief, reversal, curiosity gap, and bridge. Do not answer the likely causes in the hook.
- Diagnosis translates the metric into customer behaviour.
- Cost of inaction shows why obvious tactics are short-term or band-aid fixes before moving into the real fix path.
- Fix path must match the stage psychology, not reuse a generic checklist.
- Avoid social-post cadence such as stacked repeated "X creates risk" lines unless John explicitly asks for that rhythm.
- Do not convert planning notes into spoken inventory. "Likely Causes", "First Checks", and growth-lever notes are there to guide the thinking; the spoken script should use one mental model and one decision path, not repeated "if X..." or "you could..." ladders.
- Paid next step must match the result state. Sometimes the bridge is "this is real expertise"; sometimes, as in checkout completion, the bridge is "the simple leak fix creates room to optimise and scale, and John keeps conversion sharp while the operator grows the business."
- Do not inflate simple fixes. If the fix is mostly settings and friction removal, say that plainly and sell the next layer of value instead.
