# Checkout Myth

## Metadata

- Lead magnet: Shopify Bottleneck Analysis
- Angle: belief reversal
- Target length: 2:10-2:30
- Placement: Meta/Instagram
- Destination: `/shopify-bottleneck-analysis`
- CTA: "Run the free Shopify Bottleneck Analysis."
- Script status: approved draft; ready for production planning
- Target viewer state: looks at overall conversion rate and needs a sharper way to see where the funnel is weak

## Hook

"If you want to turn more Shopify browsers into buyers, this simple metric will make your life so much easier. I call it the Shopify Golden Ratio."

## Purpose

Create curiosity around the Shopify Golden Ratio as a simpler, more useful way to understand where an ecommerce sales funnel needs attention.

## Style Structure

This script uses the short named-mechanism framework from the Shopify Hook Framework:

1. Hook: name the desired outcome, the simple mechanism, and the promised benefit.
2. Quick reversal: "it turns out..." the usual way of looking at the problem is too blunt.
3. "So here's the trick": explain the mechanism in three simple steps.
4. Why it works: show how the blunt overall conversion rate becomes a specific funnel-stage target.
5. Lead magnet solution: "I think this is so useful that I built..." then send them to the free analysis.

## Alignment Checks

- Ad promise: compare your store to the Shopify Golden Ratio and see where the funnel needs attention.
- Landing page promise: run the free Shopify Bottleneck Analysis to find the likely weak funnel stage and immediate next steps.
- Report output: product page, cart, checkout, or no obvious major leak.
- CTA: free analysis only, not the paid diagnostic.
- Remotion continuity: same funnel rail, stage cards, lower thirds, and CTA-card treatment as the landing page and result videos.

## Spoken Script

If you want to turn more Shopify browsers into buyers, this simple metric will make your life so much easier.

I call it the Shopify Golden Ratio.

You see, most store owners go straight to the overall conversion rate.

In fact, Shopify puts it at the top of the dashboard.

And while it is an attention-grabbing number, it's not actually all that useful.

Yes, it'll tell you what's happening across your whole store.

But you get no information on where your funnel is broken or what you need to do next.

That's why we created the Golden Ratio.

Here's how it works.

Instead of one overall number, the Shopify Golden Ratio breaks your funnel down into three core buying actions.

Based on well-optimised Shopify funnels, approximately 10% of the people who visit your store should add something to the cart.

Of those 10%, about half, or 50%, should get to checkout.

And of those, about 80% should pony up some cash and actually buy something from you.

That's the Golden Ratio.

And once you've used this on your store, you'll never look at your sales funnel the same way again.

Now, the reason this works so well is because it takes the interesting-yet kind of useless conversion rate metric, and instead gives you the information you need to laser-target the exact stage in your funnel that needs the most work.

In fact, I think this is so useful that I've built a free tool so anyone can compare the Golden Ratio to their store.

All you have to do is enter some basic details, and you'll get instant clarity on your sales funnel with immediate next steps.

Every one of my clients, it's the first report I make them look at each week.

And if there's an easier or more useful way to analyse an ecommerce sales funnel, I'm not aware of it.

Click the link, see how your store compares to the Shopify Golden Ratio, and I promise, you'll never again look at your Shopify store the same way.
