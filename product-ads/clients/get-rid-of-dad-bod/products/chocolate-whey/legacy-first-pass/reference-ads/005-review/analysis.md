## What The Ad Is Doing

This format uses one large quote and a simple product shot to transfer trust. The product is not explained in detail; the quote gives the viewer a human reason to care.

## Emotional Job

- Reassurance: someone has already had a positive experience.
- Social proof: the viewer sees a real voice before product details.
- Simplicity: one statement is easier to absorb than a feature stack.
- Credibility: the sparse layout makes the claim feel restrained.

## Copy Mechanism

The quote should name a concrete lived benefit. Vague praise such as "love it" is weak; a strong quote explains what became easier, calmer, simpler, or more repeatable.

## Safe-Zone Template Rules

- Keep the quote and attribution between 17% and 35% height.
- Keep the product label visible between 38% and 64% height in Reels.
- Bottom legal/disclaimer copy is not Reels-safe; critical disclaimers must also appear in primary text or landing page.
- Do not place a CTA in the bottom 20% for Stories or bottom 35% for Reels.

## Watchouts

- Do not present a customer review unless the quote, name, and permission are verified.
- Once real reviews exist, replace the brand-proof line with a specific customer quote about repeatability, taste, or ease.
