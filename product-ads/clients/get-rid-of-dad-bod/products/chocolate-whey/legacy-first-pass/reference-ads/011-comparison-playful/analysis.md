## What The Ad Is Doing

This format uses humour and asymmetry to make one choice feel obviously better. It is less formal than a comparison table; the tilted cards make it feel native to social feeds.

## Emotional Job

- Amusement: the weaker side earns attention by being funny.
- Relief: the stronger side makes the viewer feel there is a simpler option.
- Contrast: the split background makes the decision visually immediate.
- Low-pressure persuasion: humour lowers resistance before the product proof lands.

## Copy Mechanism

The headline sets up an A/B decision. The left cards dramatise the annoying alternative. The right cards make the product-backed habit feel calmer, cleaner, and easier.

## Safe-Zone Template Rules

- Keep the `VS` headline and both product identifiers inside 27% to 36% height.
- Keep the three proof cards inside 43% to 63% height.
- For Reels, do not place important proof cards below 65% height.
- Use large type and keep card rotation modest so mobile readability survives.

## Watchouts

- Keep the joke about routine chaos, not about the viewer's body.
- Do not use emojis as production icons; replace them with designed card marks before export.
