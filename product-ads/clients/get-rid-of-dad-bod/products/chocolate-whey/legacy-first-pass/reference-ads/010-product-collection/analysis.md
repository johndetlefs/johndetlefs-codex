## What The Ad Is Doing

This format makes the product feel like the centre of a small kit. The big question captures the problem, the product collection makes the solution tangible, and the CTA gives a direct shopping action.

## Emotional Job

- Recognition: the opening question names a situation the viewer understands.
- Momentum: the kit scene makes the next action feel ready-to-go.
- Social trust: reviews or proof below the CTA reduce hesitation.
- Playfulness: bold colour and large type make the ad feel energetic.

## Copy Mechanism

The question creates the need. The subhead names the product as the answer. The CTA is visually obvious. Proof below the CTA should be real; do not invent star ratings or review counts.

## Safe-Zone Template Rules

- Move the CTA into the Reels safe rectangle, around 57% to 63% height.
- Keep headline and subhead above 34% height.
- Keep the core product pack visible above 60% height; accessory items may extend lower.
- If proof is important, put it above the CTA or directly under it inside the safe rectangle.
