# Get Rid Of Dad Bod - Meta / Instagram Image Ad Template Pack

Date: 2026-05-25
Product: Get Rid Of Dad Bod Chocolate Whey Protein Concentrate
Market: Australia
Primary audience: Middle-aged dads in a weight-loss phase who want to keep muscle, strength, and routine.

## Core Positioning

This is not a weight-loss product. The useful, safer commercial frame is:

> When calories and appetite are down, protein needs to stay up.

The product is a simple, practical protein tool for dads who are trying to get leaner without letting their strength, training routine, or muscle mass disappear during the process.

## Claims To Use

Use claims that are visible on the product label or supported by standard protein-health-claim framing:

- `30.8g protein per 40g serve`, rounded in ad copy to `31g protein per serve`.
- `Grass fed whey protein concentrate`.
- `Chocolate`.
- `1kg`.
- `25 serves`.
- `1.2g sugar per serve`.
- `Made in Australia`.
- `Protein contributes to the maintenance of muscle mass`, subject to final compliance review.

## Claims To Avoid

Avoid these in ads, image text, primary text, landing-page headlines, and thumbnails:

- Do not say or imply the product causes weight loss.
- Do not say or imply the product replaces medical care.
- Do not mention `Ozempic`, `Mounjaro`, `Wegovy`, `semaglutide`, `tirzepatide`, `GLP-1`, or `peptides` in consumer-facing ads.
- Do not use injectable pens, prescription boxes, pharmacy scenes, or doctor imagery.
- Do not use before/after body images.
- Do not write copy that asserts a personal attribute about the viewer, such as `Are you overweight?`, `On Ozempic?`, `Lost your muscle?`, or `Got a dad bod?`
- Do not promise a body outcome, timeframe, kilograms lost, muscle gained, or fat loss result.

## Source Notes

- The flat front label and rendered pack shot originally show `1KG (20 Servings)`. That is a typo. Production creative should use `25 serves` or crop/patch the serving-count area until the product render is updated.
- The back label states `25 Serving Per 1kg Packet`, `40 grams Per Serve`, and `30.8g protein` per serve. That is the working source of truth for the first ads.

## First Creative System

Build all templates in three ratios:

- `4:5` feed: `1080 x 1350`, primary build.
- `1:1` square: `1080 x 1080`, carousel/catalog-safe.
- `9:16` story/reel static: `1080 x 1920`, safe-zone aware.

Keep image text short. Let primary text carry nuance. The image should communicate one idea in under one second.

Safe-zone checking is part of the creative system, not an optional final polish step. Use the editor overlays before export: Reels-safe for any `9:16` creative that may run in Reels, Stories-safe when building Stories-only variants, and Edge guard for feed/link variants.

## Production Asset Rule

For any template that relies on arrows, icons, stickers, badges, scribbles, illustrated callouts, or similar reusable visual assets, create a dedicated asset sheet before building the layout.

This is required for the feature/benefit product hero format and any future ad format with hand-drawn or illustrative support assets. Do not hand-code final icon artwork as ad hoc SVG paths, and do not use emojis as production icons.

The required process is documented in `ad-production-process.md`. In short:

1. Use the reference ad to define the asset style.
2. Generate or source a coherent asset sheet.
3. Store the source sheet under `generated/assets/icon-sheets/`.
4. Cut selected assets into transparent individual files under `generated/assets/icons/<style-name>/`.
5. Wire the editor/template to use those assets as editable layers.
6. Use the editor for layout and ratio variants, not for inventing the artwork.
7. Trace final high-use assets into clean SVGs when full colour/stroke editing is needed.

For feature/benefit callout ads, do not ship a one-option icon set. Build at least four icon options per benefit/feature and multiple arrows per required direction, then expose them in grouped editor dropdowns.

For source-inspired reference templates, use the production asset manifest in `reference-ads/asset-requirements.md`. That file documents the required product layer, GPT Image 2.0 backgrounds, hero shots, props, and icon/sticker sets for each R01-R11 format.

Before building or revising any source-inspired template, create a forensic teardown in the matching `reference-ads/<reference-folder>/forensic-teardown.md` file. The teardown is the source-faithfulness brief: it records composition percentages, copy hierarchy, emotion, typography, asset requirements, and a side-by-side QA scorecard.

## Image Generation / Editing Direction

Use real product imagery for the product itself. Generate or source only the surrounding scene:

- Australian kitchen bench, early morning, shaker and keys.
- Garage gym or spare-room weights setup.
- Work bag / gym bag flat lay.
- Chocolate texture background.
- Neutral macro/label cards.

Do not generate alternate product packaging from scratch unless the goal is concept exploration only. Packaging text must be composited from the real approved label or manually designed.
