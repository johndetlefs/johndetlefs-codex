## Product Positioning

This is a rare Italian sour-cherry liqueur-wine from Pergola, Marche. The strongest first-pass ad angle is not "sweet dessert wine"; it is a more elevated ritual:

> Not just dessert wine. A rare Italian sour-cherry ritual.

## Claims To Use

- Vino di Visciole from Pergola, Marche.
- Sour cherry wine / sour cherry nectar.
- Made with wild `Prunus Cerasus` sour cherries.
- Steeped in local Montepulciano and Sangiovese red wine with cane sugar.
- Fermented over `4-5 months`.
- Deep ruby-red.
- Sour cherry preserve, wildflowers, dried herbs.
- Pairs with chocolate, cheese, or dessert.
- `13.5% ABV` if needed in supporting copy.

## Claims To Avoid

- No health claims.
- No confidence, romance, mood, status, or performance promises.
- No implication that alcohol improves social outcomes.
- No heavy-consumption language.
- No youth-coded visual treatment.
