# R02 - Notes / Personal Switch Adaptation Teardown

Client/product adaptation: Alessa Vino / Corrado Tonelli Visner di Pergola Selezione Vino di Visciole 2024

## Source Mechanism

R02 makes a product choice feel simple by placing it inside a personal notes-app frame. The design does not need icons, arrows, or heavy product education. The key is a first-person headline, a short checklist, and a large product visual underneath.

## Alessa Vino Emotional Job

Desired feeling:

- "This is the unusual bottle I would save for an after-dinner dessert moment."

Interest mechanism:

- `Why I'm opening Visner after dinner` translates the product into a specific occasion without promising a personal outcome.

Desire mechanism:

- The checklist gives four concrete reasons: flavour base, aroma cues, origin/tradition, and food pairing.

Anxiety reduced:

- "What is it?" -> sour cherry plus red wine.
- "Why is it special?" -> Pergola, Marche tradition.
- "When would I open it?" -> chocolate and cheese pairing after dinner.

## Visual Adaptation

Preserve from source:

- Fake notes-app top chrome.
- Large first-person headline.
- Orange check circles.
- Four short checklist rows.
- Product image under the checklist.
- Minimal white/cream background.

Adapt for Alessa:

- Use the same custom cherry-depth red background from the first Alessa ad so the campaign feels connected.
- Use cream headline/checklist text and cellar-gold check accents so the notes remain readable on the darker background.
- Keep the fake Notes chrome inside the edge guard while allowing it to sit above the stricter Stories/Reels safe rectangle.
- Keep the headline, checklist, and product-identifying bottle area inside the 9:16 Reels safe rectangle where practical.
- Keep all language culinary and product-specific for alcohol compliance.
- Use the cut-out bottle image: `/clients/alessa-vino/products/visner-di-pergola-2024/assets/product-shots/visner-di-pergola-cutout.png`.

## Acceptance Criteria

- R02 mechanism is visible before reading body copy: note chrome, first-person headline, checklist, product.
- Text remains inside safe side guards in 9:16.
- Checklist is short and readable at mobile thumbnail size.
- Product bottle is cut out and label-readable where practical.
- Alcohol copy avoids health, mood, confidence, romance, status, performance, or heavy-consumption claims.
