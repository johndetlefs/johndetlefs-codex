# 001 - Feature / Benefit Product Hero Forensic Teardown

Source file: `original.webp`
Source URL: `source-url.txt`
Observed dimensions: `480 x 854`
Primary format: `9:16` vertical
Source category: product-first supplement feature/benefit ad
Client/product adaptation: Get Rid Of Dad Bod Chocolate Whey Protein Concentrate

## 1. One-Sentence Diagnosis

This is a product-first category-reframe ad: the product pack is made to feel like the obvious centre of a simpler daily habit, while four white hand-drawn callouts remove the main objections around ingredients, taste, prep, and digestion.

## 2. Emotional Job

Desired feeling:

- "This is easy, clean, and not another complicated supplement routine."

Interest mechanism:

- The headline does not say "greens gummies." It says `Greens Reimagined`, which creates a small category twist and makes the viewer ask what has changed.

Desire mechanism:

- The yellow subhead translates the category into a habit: `Not just a supplement - an easy healthy habit.`
- The central pack is large enough to make the SKU memorable, while the surrounding callouts make it feel low-effort.

Anxiety/objection reduced:

- Ingredient quality: `60+ whole-food ingredients`
- Taste/texture: `Fruity flavor, zero grit`
- Effort: `No prep needed`
- Tolerance: `Gentle on digestion`

Proof mechanism:

- The proof is not statistical depth. It is fast objection coverage around a clear product hero.

## 3. Copy System

Exact source headline:

- `Greens Reimagined`

Exact source subhead:

- `Not just a supplement - an easy healthy habit.`

Exact source callouts:

- `60+ whole-food ingredients`
- `Fruity flavor, zero grit`
- `No prep needed`
- `Gentle on digestion`

Copy formula:

- Headline: `[category] reimagined`
- Subhead: `Not just a [category object] - an easy [desired habit].`
- Callouts: four concise objection-removers, each two to four words where possible.

Required adaptation rule:

- Preserve the category-reframe/habit pattern. If the Get Rid Of Dad Bod version uses a totally different headline shape, the ad stops feeling like this source.

Source-faithful Get Rid Of Dad Bod copy direction:

- Headline: `Protein Reimagined`
- Subhead: `Not just a powder - an easy protein habit.`
- Callouts: `31g protein`, `Low sugar chocolate`, `Grass fed whey`, `No messy prep`

Alternate product-safe headline if the category needs stronger dad-specific desire:

- `Whey Reimagined`

Avoid:

- Weight-loss promises, body-shaming, medical references, GLP-1 references, before/after language, or claims that the product creates a body outcome.

QA rule:

- Do not trust asset filenames alone. Visually confirm the arrowhead direction and the callout-to-product connection on the rendered ad against the source before marking arrow placement as complete.
- A correct R01 arrow does two jobs: it starts from the callout it belongs to and ends by pointing at the product pack. If it points in the right direction but floats away from the product edge, it fails QA.
- A correct R01 arrow also curves the same way as the source. If the tail and head are approximately correct but the bow is visually reversed, it fails QA.

Callout spacing pass:

- Source spacing intent: compact callout clusters with clear separation between icon, text, and arrow. Arrows sit close enough to the product to read as product callouts, but do not crowd the text blocks.
- Latest manual correction diagnosis: the previous pass tuned arrows separately, but the source works because each callout is a compact icon/text/arrow lockup orbiting the product. The corrected version shrinks the support elements, pulls the clusters inward toward the pack, and tightens each icon-to-text stack before placing the arrow.
- Top-left cluster: arrow, icon, and text are now a compact upper-left lockup. The icon/text moved inward and upward, and the arrow was reduced so it reads as a short product pointer rather than a separate decorative stroke.
- Top-right cluster: icon and text moved left/up as a lockup, while the arrow was reduced and moved into the gap below the text. This keeps the source-like compact right-side callout without letting the arrow dominate the text.
- Lower clusters: both lower lockups moved inward/up. The lower-right arrow is smaller and steeper than the previous pass, matching the source's tucked-in lower callout pattern more closely.
- Acceptance rule: each callout needs a readable stack of icon -> text -> arrow/product relationship. The arrow may pass near the icon/text, but the final rendered stroke must not appear to touch or interrupt the words.
- Measurement note: the arrow PNG bounding boxes include transparent padding. Use the editor measurements for coarse placement, then judge final overlap by the visible white stroke in the rendered export.

## 5. Visual System

Palette:

- Dominant saturated green background.
- Dark green/near-black headline bar.
- Warm yellow subhead and product wordmark.
- White callout text/icons/arrows.

Background texture/light:

- Smooth digital gradient with soft radial glow.
- No lifestyle photography, props, or extra texture.

Product treatment:

- Large front-facing pack, centred.
- Strong contrast against green.
- Product shadow is subtle and mostly used to separate the pack from the background, not to ground it on a table.

Typography:

- Headline: high-contrast serif, title case, large, white.
- Subhead: compact sans, small, black, medium/bold.
- Callouts: uppercase sans, white, bold, tightly line-broken.

Icons/arrows:

- White hand-drawn line icons.
- White hand-drawn curved arrows around the pack.
- Icons are simple and slightly playful, not polished corporate line icons.
- The arrows are part of the selling mechanism because they keep returning attention to the product.

Props/scene:

- None. The ad is deliberately product and callout only.

Shadows/depth:

- Product has soft separation from background.
- Header and subhead are flat UI blocks with mild rounded corners rather than dimensional cards.
