## What The Ad Is Doing

This is a mood-led product scene with a direct emotional headline and three benefit pills. The product is placed in a believable routine context, so the claim feels like part of a moment rather than a label panel.

## Emotional Job

- Relief: the headline names the mental state the product helps the viewer move toward.
- Calm control: the scene feels ordered, quiet, and repeatable.
- Fast comprehension: the three pills give the viewer a reason to care without reading a paragraph.
- Permission: the ad makes the product feel like a normal routine tool.

## Copy Mechanism

The headline personifies the problem or desired state, then the pills turn the mood into tangible benefits. The copy works because it does not begin with an ingredient; it begins with the outcome the viewer wants to feel.

## Safe-Zone Template Rules

- Keep logo, headline, benefit pills, and product label inside the Reels safe rectangle.
- Use the lower 35% only for surface, shadow, or non-essential product continuation.
- Pills should be short enough to read at mobile size and should not touch side guards.
- Avoid placing a CTA below the product unless the asset is Stories-only.
