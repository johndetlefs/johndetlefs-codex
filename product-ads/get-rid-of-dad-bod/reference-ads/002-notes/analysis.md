## What The Ad Is Doing

This format borrows the interface of a personal notes app. The design makes the ad feel less like a brand claim and more like something a real person saved for themselves after making a decision.

## Emotional Job

- Curiosity: the viewer wants to know why someone is switching.
- Borrowed confidence: the checklist implies the thinking has already been done.
- Ease: the note format makes the decision feel simple, not technical.
- Insider value: a discount or link mention creates the feeling of a small find.

## Copy Mechanism

The headline uses first-person momentum: `Why I'm switching to...`. The bullet list then stacks reasons from practical to emotional. The best bullets are short, plain, and decision-supporting, not feature dumps.

## Safe-Zone Template Rules

- Treat the fake phone chrome as decorative and keep it above the Reels safe rectangle if needed.
- Keep the headline, bullets, and product label inside the 6% side guard.
- For Reels, start the headline around 16% from the top and keep the final bullet above 40% so the product can sit safely in the middle.
- Do not put discount or CTA copy in the bottom 35% if the asset may run in Reels.
