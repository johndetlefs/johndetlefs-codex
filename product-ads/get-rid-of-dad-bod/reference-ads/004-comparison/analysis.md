## What The Ad Is Doing

This format creates desire by making the choice feel obvious. The left column is presented as the more complete, premium, intentional option; the right column is reduced to a weaker generic alternative.

## Emotional Job

- Certainty: the viewer does not need to decode a crowded category.
- Validation: the preferred choice looks smarter and better specified.
- Risk reduction: the table format feels factual and orderly.
- Status: the branded option looks like the more considered purchase.

## Copy Mechanism

The top row establishes the comparison. Each following row uses one short proof point. The left side should combine real product advantages with habit advantages. The right side should avoid unverifiable competitor claims unless the comparison is legally substantiated.

## Safe-Zone Template Rules

- In Reels-safe builds, compress the table to the central 51% height: product row plus three proof rows.
- Keep row copy centred in the safe rectangle; the bottom 35% can hold background continuation only.
- Use thick dividers only inside the safe area so overlays do not obscure the proof hierarchy.
- Avoid small legal copy in Reels unless it is repeated in primary text or landing page.
