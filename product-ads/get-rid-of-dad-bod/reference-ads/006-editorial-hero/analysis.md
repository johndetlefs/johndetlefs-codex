## What The Ad Is Doing

This format frames the product like a magazine answer to a simple need. The headline feels editorial and category-building; the product and ingredient cue then make the promise tangible.

## Emotional Job

- Aspiration: the viewer wants the tidy, intentional version of the routine.
- Wholesomeness: the ingredient cue makes the product feel less artificial.
- Completeness: "it's all in here" implies fewer decisions.
- Offer motivation: a pill-style CTA gives a clear next step without dominating the ad.

## Copy Mechanism

The headline names the bigger category need, not the product. The subhead then points into the product. A small arrow physically connects the promise to the pack, turning abstract benefit into product belief.

## Safe-Zone Template Rules

- Keep the editorial headline inside 16% to 31% height.
- Keep product and ingredient cue side by side within 36% to 63% height for Reels.
- Any offer pill should sit above 64% height if it is important.
- Fine-print offer terms are not useful in Reels-safe creative; handle terms in primary text and landing page.

> Protein basics for dads keeping training in the plan

Subhead:

> It's in the scoop.

Ingredient/product cue:

- Left: simple ingredient stack or macro tiles.
- Right: product pack.
- Arrow points from subhead to pack.

> 31g protein per serve

Primary text: `A plain-English chocolate whey protein concentrate for the part of the routine that should not need a spreadsheet: one 40g serve, 30.8g protein.`

## Watchouts

- Do not visually imply the product replaces whole foods.
- Keep the editorial tone confident but not medicinal.
