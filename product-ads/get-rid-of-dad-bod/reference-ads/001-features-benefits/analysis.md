## What The Ad Is Doing

This is a product-first feature/benefit ad. It does not rely on a lifestyle scene. The whole ad is built around immediate category recognition, a strong product pack, and four short proof points that reduce friction.

## Creative Anatomy

- Full-frame brand-colour background.
- Large headline in a dark rounded bar near the top.
- Small contrasting subhead pill underneath the headline.
- Product pack centred as the hero object.
- Four feature/benefit callouts around the product.
- Simple line icons and hand-drawn arrows guide the eye back to the product.
- Copy is short enough to read quickly on mobile.

## Why It Works

- The headline reframes the category instead of only naming the product.
- The subhead translates the product into a habit, not a supplement purchase.
- The central product keeps the SKU clear.
- Callouts answer practical objections: ingredients, taste, prep effort, digestion.
- Icons and arrows create motion without needing video.
- It works even when the viewer has no prior brand context.

## Risks / Watchouts

- Four callouts can become cluttered if the claims are too long.
- The headline needs high contrast; otherwise the ad becomes a green wall.
- This format is claim-heavy, so every callout needs to be label-supported or otherwise compliant.
- For Get Rid Of Dad Bod, avoid body-shaming or direct personal-attribute copy.

## Reusable Template

Template name: `Feature / Benefit Product Hero`

Use this when:

- The goal is fast product education.
- The product has clear label proof points.
- We want a clean ad that can run cold without relying on a person or lifestyle scene.

Layout:

1. Solid or lightly textured brand-colour background.
2. Top headline bar: category reframe.
3. Small subhead pill: habit or practical-use frame.
4. Centre product pack.
5. Four callouts around product with icons/arrows.
6. Optional bottom micro-proof line or CTA-safe note.

## Production Asset Requirement

This format must use a dedicated icon/arrow asset sheet before final layout work. The arrows and icons are part of the ad's selling mechanism; they should not be recreated as rough code-drawn SVG paths or emojis.

Required asset pass:

- Generate or source several short hand-drawn arrow variants.
- Generate or source the benefit icons as a coherent set.
- Cut the approved sheet into transparent individual assets.
- Use those assets as editor layers for placement, scaling, rotation, mirroring, and ratio adaptation.

## Comparison Notes From First Render

The original Grüns reference works because the copy is objection-led:

- `60+ whole-food ingredients` answers ingredient quality.
- `Fruity flavor, zero grit` answers taste and texture.
- `No prep needed` answers effort.
- `Gentle on digestion` answers a common supplement concern.

The first Get Rid Of Dad Bod render was too literal. `31g protein`, `low sugar`, `grass fed`, and `shaker ready` were accurate, but the headline and callouts did not create enough buyer interest for a dad who wants quality protein while keeping muscle in the plan.

The revised version moves closer to the source pattern:

- Headline reframes the benefit: `Muscle Support Without The Junk`.
- Subhead turns the product into a habit: `A simple protein habit when appetite drops.`
- Callouts answer concerns: protein amount, chocolate/low-sugar taste, whey quality, and easy prep.

Compliance note:

Do not mention Ozempic, Mounjaro, GLP-1, injections, medical treatment, or weight-loss results in this ad. The claim frame is protein consistency and muscle-maintenance support, not weight loss.
