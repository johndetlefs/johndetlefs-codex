# Ecommerce Buying Cycle Hooks

## Hook Strategy

Use "bait-and-switch" hooks: start with something the viewer already agrees with, then hit them with a reversal that breaks the pattern and creates tension.

The structure:

1. **Agreement**: say the obvious true thing they will nod along with.
2. **Reversal**: add the uncomfortable or surprising truth.
3. **Bridge**: explain that the video will show why it happens and what to check.

Good hooks should feel like:

- "Yes, that's true."
- "Hang on, what do you mean?"
- "Actually, I should watch this."

For this guide, the hook territory is:

- Customers want to support local brands, but they still need to trust the store.
- Sending abandoned cart emails is smart, unless the message does not match why the customer left.
- Getting more traffic sounds useful, unless the store is losing people before they are ready to buy.
- A good-looking store helps, unless it fails to answer the questions customers need answered.

Use these hooks across the main video opening, Shorts/Reels, Meta Ads, and YouTube title/thumbnail tests.

## Main Video Opening Candidates

### Option 1: Local Support Reversal

Most people on the Central Coast want to support local brands. I genuinely believe that.

But they do not want to feel like they are buying from Dodgy Brothers.

If your Shopify store feels unclear, risky, hard to navigate, or like nobody is quite sure what happens after they enter their credit card details, that local goodwill disappears pretty quickly.

So today we are going to look at the ecommerce buying cycle: the steps your store needs to take a customer through before they are comfortable buying from you.

### Option 2: Local Brand Trust Gap

People do want to buy from small local brands.

They just do not necessarily trust your store yet.

That sounds harsh, but online the customer cannot look you in the eye, ask a question over the counter, or see whether the shop feels real. Your Shopify store has to build that confidence for you.

### Option 3: Abandoned Cart Reversal

Sending an email to someone who left your checkout is a fantastic idea.

Unless you send the wrong message at the wrong time.

If the customer left because they did not trust your delivery timing, did not understand sizing, or were not sure the product was right for them, a discount may not fix the sale. The message has to match where they are in the buying cycle.

### Option 4: Traffic Reversal

Most Shopify stores could use more traffic.

But more traffic will not fix a store that is losing people before they are ready to buy.

If customers cannot find the right product, trust the business, answer their objections, or check out easily, more traffic just means more people leaving.

### Option 5: Good Website Reversal

Making your Shopify store look better is usually a good idea.

But a prettier store can still fail if it does not do the sales job.

In a physical shop, someone can answer questions, reassure the customer, and ask for the sale. Online, your store has to do that work by itself.

### Option 6: Discount Reversal

Discounts can absolutely help ecommerce sales.

But sending a discount when the customer actually needs an answer can make your store feel cheaper, not more trustworthy.

Before you offer money off, you need to know whether they are stuck on price, trust, delivery, sizing, product fit, or checkout friction.
